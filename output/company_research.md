# Company Overview
Leica Microsystems, a Danaher operating company, is a global leader in microscopy, imaging and analysis solutions. With over 175 years of heritage (including legacy brands like Leitz, Wild and Cambridge Instruments), Leica develops and manufactures precision optical and digital instruments for life sciences, diagnostics, biotechnology, material sciences and surgical specialties. Headquartered in Wetzlar, Germany, Leica Microsystems operates six major production and R&D sites (Germany, Austria, Switzerland, USA, Singapore) and is represented in 100+ countries through direct sales, service organizations and distribution partners.

# Role Context and Product Involvement
As a **Senior Firmware Engineer** on the Research & Development Medical team in Singapore, you will:  
- Design and deliver embedded firmware for Leica’s market-leading surgical and lab microscopes (Linux, RTOS, embedded Windows).  
- Lead the full firmware lifecycle: requirements engineering, architecture design, coding (C/C++), unit and system testing, verification/validation, maintenance and field-return analysis.  
- Uphold medical-device quality and safety standards (US/EU regulations) and enforce coding standards through peer reviews.  
- Collaborate cross-functionally with hardware engineers, software teams, quality management and regulatory affairs under the Danaher Business System framework.

# Likely Interview Topics
- Embedded development fundamentals: RTOS vs. embedded Linux architectures, interrupt handling, memory management  
- Proficiency in C/C++ for safety-critical firmware, unit testing frameworks and debugging tools (JTAG, logic analyzers)  
- Firmware lifecycle and process: requirements traceability, UML/architecture design, Agile/Stage-Gate, design reviews  
- Medical-device regulations: IEC 62304, ISO 13485, FDA 510(k) process, risk management (ISO 14971)  
- Danaher Business System: Lean principles, Kaizen events, continuous improvement metrics  
- Cross-functional collaboration: integrating firmware with optical/mechanical subsystems and AI software modules  
- Problem-solving scenarios: root cause analysis of field returns, implementing corrective and preventive actions (CAPA)
